# Target Meetings Prioritization for Workback Plan Platform

**Author**: Chin-Yew Lin  
**Created**: November 13, 2025

## Prioritization Framework

### Scoring Criteria (100 points total)
- **Value** (35 points): Business impact, decision criticality, cost of failure
- **Frequency** (25 points): How often these meetings occur across enterprises
- **Planning Ease** (20 points): Predictability of structure, availability of templates
- **Quality Validation** (20 points): Ease of checking if preparation was adequate

---

## Priority Rankings

### Tier 1: Phase 1 Launch Targets (Score 7.5+)

#### 1. Quarterly Business Reviews (QBRs) - **Score: 8.75**
**Frequency**: Very High (4x/year per business unit)  
**Value**: High ($50K-200K per meeting)  
**Planning Ease**: High (standardized format)  
**Quality Check**: High (data availability requirements)

**Why Top Priority**:
- Predictable quarterly cadence across all Fortune 500 companies
- Standardized format: financial review → operational metrics → strategic initiatives
- Clear quality assertions: financial data 5+ days early, <3 last-minute deck changes
- High ROI: 31% faster QBR prep time documented (Bain & Company)
- 53% of QBRs delayed due to poor coordination (McKinsey)

**Workback Plan Template Elements**:
- T-21 days: Data collection begins (finance, operations, sales)
- T-14 days: First draft deck with preliminary numbers
- T-7 days: Executive pre-read and feedback
- T-3 days: Final deck lock, no content changes
- T-1 day: Tech check, backup plans

**Quality Assertions**:
- ✅ Financial data finalized ≥5 days before meeting
- ✅ Pre-read distributed ≥3 business days early
- ✅ <3 slide changes in final 24 hours
- ✅ All executive reviewers acknowledged pre-read
- ✅ Backup presenter identified

---

#### 2. Board of Directors Reviews - **Score: 8.25**
**Frequency**: High (4-6x/year)  
**Value**: Very High ($200K+ per meeting)  
**Planning Ease**: Medium-High (formal but varied)  
**Quality Check**: High (governance requirements)

**Why High Priority**:
- 72% of executives report inadequate prep time for board meetings (Deloitte)
- Average cost per board meeting: $204,000 including executive time
- Governance mandates make quality measurable (e.g., Sarbanes-Oxley compliance)
- High consequence of failure: fiduciary risk, strategic misalignment

**Workback Plan Template Elements**:
- T-30 days: Board packet outline approved by Chair
- T-21 days: First draft materials (financials, strategy updates)
- T-14 days: Executive committee review
- T-10 days: Legal/compliance review
- T-7 days: Final board packet distribution (often required by bylaws)
- T-2 days: Board member briefings for complex topics

**Quality Assertions**:
- ✅ Board packet ≥7 days before meeting (governance requirement)
- ✅ All financial statements auditor-reviewed
- ✅ Legal sign-off on compliance sections
- ✅ 100% of board members confirmed receipt
- ✅ Executive briefings completed for new/complex items

#### 3. Executive Sales Presentations - **Score: 7.95**
**Frequency**: Very High (weekly-monthly for large accounts)  
**Value**: Very High ($500K-50M+ deal value)  
**Planning Ease**: Medium-High (discovery → demo → proposal structure)  
**Quality Check**: High (win rate, customer feedback)

**Why High Priority**:

**Quality Assertions**:
- ✅ ≥2 discovery calls completed documenting customer needs
- ✅ Solution validated by technical team
- ✅ Internal dry run completed with feedback incorporated
- ✅ Pricing/terms approved by leadership
- ✅ Backup presenter and technical support identified
- ✅ Customer-specific ROI model prepared

### Tier 3: Phase 3 Enterprise-Only (Score <6.5)

#### 7. M&A Deal Reviews - **Score: 6.35**
**Frequency**: Low (1-3 major deals/year for active acquirers)  
**Value**: Extreme ($10M-10B+ deal value)  
**Planning Ease**: Low (highly variable, confidential)  
**Quality Check**: Medium (due diligence checklists, but outcomes lag years)

**Why Lower Priority (Despite High Value)**:
- Low frequency: only ~20% of Fortune 500 actively pursuing M&A annually
- High variability: every deal is unique, templates less applicable
- Confidentiality constraints limit information sharing
- Long outcome timeline makes quality validation difficult
- Better as custom/enterprise tier offering

**Workback Plan Template Elements**:
- T-90 days: Due diligence team kickoff
- T-60 days: Financial, legal, technical workstreams in parallel
- T-45 days: Integration planning begins
- T-30 days: Board presentation draft #1
- T-21 days: Risk assessment and mitigation plans
- T-14 days: Final due diligence reports
- T-7 days: Board materials finalized
- T-2 days: Executive alignment on recommendation

**Quality Assertions**:
- ✅ All due diligence workstreams completed ≥7 days before board meeting
- ✅ Integration plan covers ≥80% of identified risks
- ✅ Financial model validated by external advisors
- ✅ Legal reviewed all regulatory/antitrust implications
- ✅ Cultural assessment completed
- ✅ Day 1 integration plan documented

## Quality Validation Framework

### How We Validate Each Meeting Type

#### QBRs - Validation Criteria
1. **Data Readiness**: Financial data finalized ≥5 days before meeting
2. **Pre-read Compliance**: Distributed ≥3 business days early
3. **Deck Stability**: <3 slide changes in final 24 hours
4. **Stakeholder Review**: All executives acknowledged pre-read
5. **Backup Plans**: Alternate presenter identified

#### Board Reviews - Validation Criteria
1. **Governance Compliance**: Board packet distributed ≥7 days early (often legally required)
2. **Audit Review**: Financial statements reviewed by auditors
3. **Legal Sign-off**: Compliance sections approved by counsel
4. **Acknowledgment**: 100% board member receipt confirmation
5. **Briefings**: Executive briefings for complex topics completed

#### Sales Presentations - Validation Criteria
1. **Discovery**: ≥2 customer discovery calls documented
2. **Solution Validation**: Technical team validated approach
3. **Internal Review**: Dry run completed with feedback
4. **Approval**: Pricing/terms approved by leadership
5. **Readiness**: Backup resources and tech support ready
6. **Value Prop**: Customer-specific ROI model prepared

#### Product Launches - Validation Criteria
1. **Readiness Scorecards**: All departments submitted ≥7 days before go/no-go
2. **Beta Completion**: Pilot program with ≥50 users completed
3. **Training**: Sales training ≥90% completion rate
4. **Documentation**: Support materials published
5. **Rollback Plan**: Contingency plan tested
6. **War Room**: Launch day command center staffed

#### Compliance Reviews - Validation Criteria
1. **Evidence Collection**: 100% of required evidence ≥7 days early
2. **Internal Review**: Gaps identified and remediated
3. **Policy Currency**: All policies reviewed and current
4. **Officer Sign-off**: Compliance officer approved
5. **Audit Trail**: Complete documentation chain
6. **Remediation**: Plan for outstanding items documented

#### Investor Relations - Validation Criteria
1. **Data Review**: Financial data auditor-reviewed ≥7 days early
2. **Legal Clearance**: All forward-looking statements approved (Reg FD)
3. **Rehearsal**: ≥2 full rehearsals completed
4. **Q&A Prep**: Prep book covers ≥20 anticipated analyst questions
5. **Press Release**: Pre-cleared by legal and IR team
6. **Backup**: Technology and alternate presenters ready

#### M&A Reviews - Validation Criteria
1. **Due Diligence**: All workstreams completed ≥7 days before board
2. **Integration Plan**: Covers ≥80% of identified risks
3. **Financial Validation**: Model reviewed by external advisors
4. **Legal Review**: Regulatory/antitrust implications assessed
5. **Cultural Assessment**: Completed and documented
6. **Day 1 Plan**: Integration plan for first 100 days
